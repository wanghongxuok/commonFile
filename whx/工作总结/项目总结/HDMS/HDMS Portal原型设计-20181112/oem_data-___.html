OEM Data

Overview

OEM Content

OEM data is customized data in HD Map which is provided by an OEM or one of its agents. It has multiple purposes as defined by the OEM owner. The data can be used to assist in the decision-making process of autonomous driving, improve the drivers experience, or for internal failure and diagnostics analysis. NavInfo provides a direct channel via an overlay layer(s) for OEM functionality and is not responsible for the relevant software or content only for the generic editing functionality.   

According to update requirements and OEM frequency needs, there are three types of OEM specific data: Semi-Permanent layer, Semi-Dynamic Layer, Dynamic Layer.

Application scenarios

HDMS provides a complete solution for customized data service.

OEM raw data cannot be applied directly to a safety function system (e.g. autonomous driving path planning) due to its source, format, etc. In order to meet the flexibility requirements of OEMs, NavInfo provides a series of services such as map matching, editing and compiling to enable OEMs to continuously enrich the content of HD Map data and further improve the safety and reliability of autonomous driving.

Dynamic Layer

• Update frequency: low

• Update period: monthly

• Contents: static information about the road, such as visibility of a marking for a specific sensor

• Update frequency: medium

• Update period: daily

• Contents: information about where HAD should not be used, such as potholes and lane grooves, coefficient of friction

• Update frequency: high

• Update period: as needed

• Contents: information with high frequency of change, such as weather or current road conditions
